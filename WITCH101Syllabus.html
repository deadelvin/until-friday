Course Overview

This course explores the origins and sociocultural contexts of magic, tracing European traditions from ancient civilizations to modern-day practices. We will examine rituals, symbols, and belief systems through a critical and historical lens.

Weekly Schedule

Week 1: Ancient European Magic – Foundational Texts

Oct 1 – 5, 2025

Readings:
  • Scot, Reginald. 1989. The Discoverie of Witchcraft. Courier Corporation.
  • Tucker, Neely. 2019. “The (Cursed?) Original Book of Witchcraft | Timeless.” Library of Congress.
  • Lévi, É., & Waite, A.E. 1897. The Mysteries of Magic. K. Paul, Trench, Trübner. (Parts I & II)
Watch:
  • "The Operation of All Wonderfull Things:" Magic and the Occult at McMaster University Library
  • The History Of Magic (2023) | Full Documentary
Learning Outcomes:

Explore the origins of European magic.

Homework:

Journal your current beliefs about and personal history with magic. Reading notes.

Suggested Readings:
  • Boulton, Richard. 1715. A Compleat History of Magick, Sorcery, and Witchcraft. E. Curll, J. Pemberton; and W. Taylor.
  • Glanvill, Joseph & Horneck, Anthony. 1700. Saducismus Triumphatus. A.L. and sold.
